About Us

This is how we roll.

Infinite loop is the only club in KJSIEIT dedicated towards developing our competitive coding skills and working towards cultivating the coding culture of our college. Our team doesn't have a hierarchy. We don't judge people, we let the compilers do that job ;)

Collaborative Problem Solving

Our team members attempt all the major coding competitions such as CodeChef’s Long Challenge, Google’s Kickstart, Facebook Hackercup and ACM-ICPC. After these competitions, our members come together to discuss their ways of approaching each problem. This encourages us to view problem statements from different perspectives which in turn leads us to the most efficient solution.

Grand Contests

We believe that a fitting exposure of new or casual coders to CP is essential to ignite the developer spark in them. This was our main motivation behind the ‘Somaiya Game of Codes’. SGOC, an ICPC-styled pan-Mumbai inter-undergraduate-college programming competition, is the annual programming contest of KJSIEIT organized by our club.

Weekly Interactive Sessions

One of the main goals of our club is to make a platform for coders to interact with each other. Subsequently, we keep meetings for the members to discuss out of the box problem statements twice a week. This welcomes a healthy conversation between coders and helps new coders make harder concepts comprehensible to them.

Project Code

Aiming to encourage coders to improve their skills during the lockdown, we organized the ‘Project Code’ competition series which is the biggest online, global, open of all, coding competition in our college. In the face of changing circumstances, our team was able to secure the success of this event series. Moreover, this event is not restricted to any age-group or nationality which invites a wide spectrum of coders to be able to participate

Build from Scratch

Our latest venture has been motivating our members to apply their knowledge by building projects. This brings out their creativity and boosts the teamwork between members while improving our skills at the same time. We as a coding club realize that having out of the box projects under one's belt is as improtant as being sound in DSA and here at Infinite Loop, we are prepared for that.

Amey Noolkar Founder, Infinite Loop

The Infinite Loop began as a group of Freshers looking to brush-up their coding skills for curricular requirements. Soon enough, we found ourselves as novice explorers of competitive coding and decided to fill the void of a coding club at our institute. With this, the Infinite Loop officially became a platform for competitive coding. From such humble foundations, the journey of Infinite Loop to host the SGOC – one of the largest pan-Mumbai programming competitions, within a span of three years is incredibly phenomenal! It reaffirms my belief that with a minimal hierarchy and maximal involvement, the Infinite Loop has what it takes to quickly mould itself and remain relevant in a singularly turbulent and disruption-prone field such as Computer Science, and enlighten the path for many more young programmers to come.
